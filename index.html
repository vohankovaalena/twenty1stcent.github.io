The Infrastructure Gap

And in participatory grant-making, we've built something nobody else has.

Participatory grant-making is no longer a fringe idea. Foundations across the UK are committing to community voice in their funding decisions. But the tools haven't kept up with the ambition.

Right now, many organisations committed to participatory approaches are managing the process through spreadsheets, manual eligibility checks, and facilitated video calls. One intermediary funder we work with processed over 7,500 eligibility responses by hand with a team of three. The dedication is extraordinary. The infrastructure gap is unsustainable.

Existing grant management platforms were designed for administration, not participation. They can track a grant through its lifecycle, but they can't enable a community panel to make an informed funding decision. So we built something that can — and the way we built it matters as much as what it does.

01

Technology That Shifts Power

Our participatory grant-making platform, Communa, is purpose-built for funders who want community voice at the centre of their decisions — not as a box-ticking exercise, but as the engine of how funding flows.

Communa uses AI to handle the operational weight of participatory processes: eligibility screening, application analysis, community panel support, and decision documentation. It doesn't replace human judgment. It creates the conditions where human judgment can focus on what matters.
